Days & Weeks

The six days of the week are, in order:

  1. Dolgos, day of stone
  2. Naugos, day of water
  3. Brogos, day of beasts
  4. Śyrgos, the people's day
  5. Draxigos, the King's day
  6. Telugos, the Gods' day

The date is given by the number of occurrences of the current day (including the current day), e.g. today is the {{today.date.week | ordinal}} {{today.date.day.name}} of the year so far.

Years & Epochs

There are 56 weeks (336 days) in a normal year, though the concept of a week starting and ending is not familiar to the users of this calendar. The phrase 'next week' typically means 'exactly 6 days from now'. This calendar also has no months (in our calendar, months are derived from the phases of the moon, but this world has two), but a typical year is split into 4 vutalan (quarters) of 14 weeks (84 days) each.

To account for calendrical drift, every 20th year is called a 'long year' and has a fifth vutalan, for a total of 70 weeks (420 days). This marks the end of the current 20-year period.

Every 10th long year is one week shorter than normal (69 weeks; 414 days) and concludes the 200-year epoch, after which the epoch number is incremented, and the year number resets to 1. Such 'terminus years' consist of four regular vutalan followed by a shorter 13-week vutalan, which causes many arguments about payment schedules.

The current year is given by the epoch followed the the year, separated by a comma, e.g. {{today.date.year.epoch}},{{today.date.year.year}}.

Seasons

Due to the severity of the calendar's drift, the start date of each season varies dramatically over the course of each 20-year period, but each of those periods consistently start in early summer, and the last year of each period starts in early spring.
